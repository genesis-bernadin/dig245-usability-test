METHODOLOGY

In this assignment, we assessed the usability of the North Carolina’s Department of Movtor Vehicles (DMV) website. This site serves as the official online platform for NC residents to receive information on relevant automotive services, such as driver licenses and vehicle registrations. Given the neccessity of DMV’s, we examined the accessibility and intuitiveness of the website’s features.


We had two participants perform the following tasks and timed their completion:

  • Make an appointment
  • Locate Hours
  • Order a replacement license/duplicate ID
  • Access License Services
  • Send a message









    • DATA ANALYSIS

    Overall, each participant had varied results with performing the desired tasks.


    Participant 1 experienced difficulty with locating the hours and sending a message quickly. We notated that they scrolled signifigantly to seek out the answer to the requested task, which we believe affected their perfomance.

    RECOMMENDATIONS

    Based on our assessments of the particpants, we conclude that the NC DMV website can be improved through the following recommendations.


    For one, basic infomration such as the hours, location, and contact information should be placed at the footer. This could be helpful for users who would rather scroll to find information as opposed to clicking through different pages.

    Likewise, this website could benefit from implementing a more prominent header, such that the “Home” and/or subsequent pages are easier to locate.


    Ultimately, the website’s current design is fairly intutitive, however, with these slight modifications it would perform better on the usability scale.
